Fall Out Boy
Fall Out Boy is an American rock band formed in Wilmette, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago, in 2001. The band features lead vocalist Patrick Stump, bassist Pete Wentz, lead guitarist Joe Trohman, and drummer Andy Hurley. Emerging from Chicago's hardcore punk scene, they began as a pop-punk side project created by Wentz and Trohman. Stump joined shortly thereafter, and the group experienced a series of drummers before Hurley became a member. Their debut album, Take This to Your Grave (2003), achieved underground success and helped the band build a dedicated fan base through relentless touring and engaging performances. (Excerpt adapted from Wikipedia)
Weezer
Weezer is an American rock band formed in Los Angeles, California, in 1992. The lineup has included Rivers Cuomo (lead vocals, guitar, keyboards), Patrick Wilson (drums, backing vocals), Brian Bell (guitar, keyboards, backing vocals), and Scott Shriner (bass guitar, keyboards, backing vocals). They have sold over 10 million albums in the U.S. and more than 35 million globally. After signing with Geffen Records in 1993, Weezer released their acclaimed self-titled debut album, commonly known as the Blue Album, in May 1994. Their unique sound and relatable lyrics have resonated with fans, solidifying their place in music history. (Excerpt adapted from Wikipedia)
